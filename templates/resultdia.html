Diabetes Management Recommendations

What To Do

  • Monitor blood glucose levels regularly as advised by your doctor
  • Follow a balanced diabetic diet plan with controlled carbohydrate intake
  • Exercise regularly - aim for 150 minutes of moderate activity per week
  • Take insulin or diabetes medications exactly as prescribed
  • Check your feet daily for cuts, blisters or swelling
  • Maintain a healthy weight through diet and exercise
  • Stay well-hydrated with water and sugar-free beverages
  • Get regular A1C tests and diabetes check-ups
  • Keep a diabetes management diary or use tracking apps

What To Avoid

  • Sugary foods and drinks including regular soda
  • Skipping meals or going too long without eating
  • Large portions of starchy foods like white rice and bread
  • Fruit juices and sweetened beverages
  • Processed snacks and baked goods
  • Foods high in saturated and trans fats
  • Alcohol on an empty stomach
  • Being inactive for long periods
  • Ignoring blood sugar monitoring

Important Note

If you experience any of these diabetes emergency signs, seek immediate medical attention:

  • Very high blood sugar (over 250 mg/dL) with severe thirst and frequent urination
  • Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
  • Fruity-smelling breath and confusion (signs of ketoacidosis)
  • Severe drowsiness or lethargy
  • Nausea and vomiting that won't stop
